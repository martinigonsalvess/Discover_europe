Settlers of Europe:

The first humans are thought to have arrived in Europe around 37,020 years ago. They first started to develop trade and technology many years later around 4720 years ago during the bronze age. However, the technology then was not how we know it today mostly focusing on metallurgy and agriculture. In addition A few of the ethnic groups had a major influence on the region such as the greek tribes and the germanic and italic (or Roman) people. The population in the region was then vastly affected by the Vikings, Mongols, and a few other settlers.

Historical Architecture:

Architecture is a huge attraction for tourists nowadays, and Europe contains five out of the top ten tourist destination in the world. France being the top tourist destination in the world bringing in around 90 million international tourists a year as it contains landmarks such as the Eiffel tower. A few other noteworthy places are the Alhambra in Spain, the Acropolis in Greece, and the Duomo in italy which is the image shown above. Furthermore these places are popular due to their innovative and creative architecture especially for the period they were built.

Modern Day Europe:

Nowadays there are about 50 countries in Europe with a total population of over 742 million people, however of these countries only 44 have their capital within the European continent. Which makes it one of the most densely populated continents in the world! It is the second smallest continent in size but the third largest in population. It also houses the European Union which contains 27 of these nations as members.
